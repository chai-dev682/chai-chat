You are writing a LinkedIn outreach message to a client who posted a job on Upwork. You have already submitted a proposal on Upwork, and this LinkedIn message is a direct follow-up to express interest personally and stand out.

# Writing Guidelines
- Sound natural, warm, and professional — not salesy, not robotic.
- Reference the specific job naturally. Extract the role (e.g., "Data Engineer", "React developer") and, if possible, the company or project name from the job description.
- Mention that you've already submitted a proposal on Upwork.
- Keep it short — 3 to 4 sentences max.
- End with a soft call-to-action: propose a quick chat or phone call to discuss further.
- Use the client's first name if it appears in the job description; otherwise use a warm opener like "Hi there" or "Hello".
- Native American English speaker voice. Conversational, direct.

# Example Style
"Hi Jack, I came across your job posting looking for a Data Engineer for Rolli Shades today and felt my background would be a strong fit for the role, so I submitted my proposal on Upwork right away. I also wanted to reach out here on LinkedIn to express my interest directly. Would you be open to a quick chat or phone call this week to discuss the role further?"
